Causes of Climate Change

The Greenhouse Effect

The greenhouse effect is explained in the name itself! The earth is surrounded by a bubble! Not a real bubble but a gas bubble . You don't need to be worried about the name of this layer. But for the nerds out there, it is called the OZONE LAYER. When the sun shines onto the earth, the heat from the sun is trapped inside this layer. The earth’s atmosphere has always acted like a greenhouse to capture the sun’s heat, ensuring that the earth has been warm enough for the new emergence of life forms including humans. So lets answer an important question! What is global warming? We'll simply put it, global warming is the slow increase in the average temperature of the earth’s atmosphere because of an increased amount of heat remaining in the ozone layer.Without the greenhouse effect the earth would be very cold. Global warming, however, is the equivalent of an actual greenhouse with high efficiency glass placed the wrong side up. Weird, right? This causes excess heat to be trapped in the earth causing major changes to the earths climate.

Burning Fossil Fuels

So what causes global warming? One major factor is the burning of fossil fuels. According to wikepedia "fossil fuels are a fuel formed by natural processes, such as anaerobic decomposition of buried dead organisms, containing energy originating in ancient photosynthesis. Such organisms and their resulting fossil fuels typically have an age of millions of years, and sometimes more than 650 million years". Burning these fuels causes an increase in the amount of carbon dioxide gas in the atmosphere. This gas likes to trap the suns heat even more, further warming the planet leading to the greenhouse effect and ultimately causing a major role in global warming! Intersting right?

Deforestation

Deforestationis the chopping down trees in large amounts.It is the second leading cause of global warming, first being the burning of fossil fuels causing excess greenhouse gases.Deforestation produces about 24% of global greenhouse gas emissions The earth is covered by 31% of forests.That is alot of forest. The Amazon Rainforest is the largest forest in the world. Scientist say that deforestation in rainforests adds more CO2 to the atmosphere than the total sum of all the cars and trucks on the world’s roads. In countries, like Brazil and Indonesia, deforestation is by far the main source of greenhouse. gas emissions.The livelihoods of 1.6 billion people depend on forests.Forests are home to 80% of the world’s wildlife according to the International Union of Concerned Scientists.The forests are cut down for food, space for cattle farming to feed the meat eaters on this planet. Also for timber to build homes etc. to cater for humans growing needs for food and space.

Fast Fashion

Fast fashion according to wikipedia is "a contemporary term used by fashion retailers for designs that move from catwalk quickly to capture current fashion trends. A second, critical definition adds that fast fashion is not only about quickly moving from runway to store to consumer, but also to the garbage" Right now the pricing of clothes is extremely cheap so people are able to buy new clothing extremely fast and replcae them even faster.It has become the norm in first world countries. Cheap labouris carried out in the third world, where work conditions are extreme and workers work excessively long hours just to get paid less then minimun wage per month. This is called sweatshop conditions. Large fashion retailers are able to make clothes with cheap labour and sell them to make huge profit margins. RTE says "it used to take six months to bring new designs to the store, but the fast fashion brands manage to bring interpretations of catwalk designs to stores today in a minimum of two to three weeks." Aswell as being a violoation of human rights, ast fashion includes its negative environmental impact, water pollution, the use of toxic chemicals and increasing levels of textile waste.The fast fashion industries account for approximately 8% of the world's greenhouse gas emissions.

Meat and Dairy

Warning, this may trigger the meat eaters reading this piece! Guess what? A mostly plant-based diet can actually help save the environment and reduce the effects of climate change! To create those hearty meaty meals , forests are cut down, harmful pesticides are used and the poor animals have to die.BBC says "A major report on land use and climate change says the West's high consumption of meat and dairy produce is fuelling global warming. But scientists and officials stopped short of explicitly calling on everyone to become vegan or vegetarian. They said that more people could be fed using less land if individuals cut down on eating meat. The document, prepared by 107 scientists for the UN's Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC), says that if land is used more effectively, it can store more of the carbon emitted by humans." A quarter of the global emissions come from food production and more than half comes from the production of animal products.

Population growth

The population is growing faster than it ever has. By the time you read this paragraph 250 babies will be born!More people means more global emissions. "A 2009 study of the relationship between population growth and global warming determined that the “carbon legacy” of just one child can produce 20 times more greenhouse gas than a person will save by driving a high-mileage car, recycling, using energy-efficient appliances and light bulbs, etc. Each child born in the United States will add about 9,441 metric tons of carbon dioxide to the carbon legacy of an average parent.Under current conditions, a child born & raised in the United States will be responsible for almost seven times the carbon emissions of a child born & in China and 168 times the impact of a child born in Bangladesh."
